The Basic Structure of Excel

Excel is organized into a grid composed of cells, rows, and columns, forming spreadsheets where you can organize, analyze, and store data.

[Image: Screenshot of the Excel interface showing cells, rows, and columns]

Cells

Intersection between a row and a column. Each cell has a unique address (such as A1, B5) and can contain data, formulas, or functions.

Rows

Horizontal sets of cells identified by numbers (1, 2, 3...). Excel has over 1 million rows per sheet.

Columns

Vertical sets of cells identified by letters (A, B, C... up to XFD). There are 16,384 columns in a modern spreadsheet.

Selecting Cells, Rows, and Columns

Mastering selection techniques is essential for working efficiently in Excel.

[Image: Visual examples of selections in Excel]

Types of Selection

Simple selection: Click on a cell to select it
Range selection: Drag your mouse over multiple cells or hold Shift while navigating with the arrow keys
Selecting an entire row/column: Click on the row number or column letter.
Selecting the entire sheet: Click the triangle between the row and column headers or press Ctrl+A
Quick tip: To select non-contiguous cells, hold down the Ctrl key while clicking on the desired cells.

Efficient Navigation

Knowing the navigation methods will save you time and improve your productivity in Excel.

Quick Navigation Methods

Go to a specific cell F5
Move to the beginning of the row Home
Move to the first cell (A1) Ctrl + Home
Move to the last cell with data Ctrl + End
Move between sheets Ctrl + Page Up/Page Down
Productivity tip: Use the F2 key to edit a cell directly without double-clicking. Press Enter to confirm changes or Esc to cancel.

Entering Information

Learning how to enter data efficiently is crucial for working productively in Excel.

[Image: Example of data entry in Excel]

Data Types

Numbers: Numeric values for calculations (may include date, currency, and percentage formats)
Text: Tags, descriptions, or any non-numeric information
Formulas: Calculations that begin with an equal sign (=)
Functions: Predefined formulas for performing specific operations
Quick method: To enter the same information in multiple cells, select all the cells, type the data, and press Ctrl+Enter.

Productivity Tips

These tips will help you work smarter and faster in Excel.

AutoComplete

Drag the fill handle (the little square in the lower-right corner of the selection) to automatically copy data or complete series.

Custom lists

Excel can recognize and automatically complete series such as days of the week, months, or custom lists.

Copy format

Use the Format Painter (brush icon) to quickly apply the same format to multiple cells.

[Image: Example of autocomplete and autofill controller in action]
Advanced tip: To quickly select a large range of data, click on the first cell and then hold Shift while clicking on the last cell. Excel will select the entire range in between.
