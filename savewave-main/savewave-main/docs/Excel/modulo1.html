What is a spreadsheet?

  • A giant grid filled with rows and columns. Each ‚Äúbox‚Äù is called a cell.
  • In each cell, you can enter numbers, text, or formulas that automatically perform calculations.
  • It's like a powerful calculator, a smart notebook, and a simple database, all in one.
  • The best-known programs are Microsoft Excel, Google Sheets, and LibreOffice Calc.

Historical evolution:

VisiCalc (1979)

First digital spreadsheet, created by Dan Bricklin and Bob Frankston

Lotus 1-2-3 (1983)

Popularized PC spreadsheets with built-in charting capabilities

Excel (1985)

It revolutionized with its graphical interface and became the industry standard.

  • Originally, sheets of paper with tables were used for financial calculations.
  • In the 1970s, VisiCalc, the first digital spreadsheet, emerged and revolutionized data management.
  • In the 1980s, Lotus 1-2-3 became popular.
  • In the mid-1980s, Microsoft launched Excel, which became the market leader thanks to its graphical interface and advanced features.
