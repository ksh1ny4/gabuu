MODULE 2 - Secure Passwords and Authentication

Objective: Apply best practices for creating and managing strong passwords, and learn about two-factor authentication tools to reinforce secure access to digital platforms.

Characteristics of a strong password

A strong password is essential for protecting personal information and preventing unauthorized access. The more complex and longer a password is, the more difficult it will be for attackers to crack it using techniques such as brute force or automated dictionaries.

A secure password must meet the following criteria:

  • Length: Ideally, 12 characters or more.
  • Variety of characters: Include uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special symbols.
  • Avoid predictable patterns: Do not use sequences such as ‚Äú123456,‚Äù ‚Äúabcdef,‚Äù or combinations such as ‚Äúqwerty.‚Äù
  • Do not use personal information: Birthdates, names, nicknames, ID numbers, etc., should be avoided.

Example of an insecure password: Ana2008 Example of a secure password: bL7lp$9kMv#4qR A secure password acts as a strong lock for our digital accounts.

Common mistakes when creating passwords

Many security breaches do not occur due to technical failures, but rather due to human error in password management. Some of the most common mistakes are:

  • Using short or simple passwords. Example: ‚Äú1234,‚Äù ‚Äúpassword,‚Äù ‚Äúhello123.‚Äù
  • Repeating the same password across multiple accounts. If one platform is compromised, all accounts with that same password are at risk.
  • Using obvious personal information. Such as pet names, important dates, or well-known nicknames. These passwords are easy to guess for someone who knows you or researches your social media profile.
  • Storing passwords in unsafe places. Such as cell phone notes, unprotected documents, or chat messages. It is important to avoid leaving passwords exposed.

These errors make it easier for malicious individuals to access private accounts without the need for advanced technical knowledge.

The importance of not reusing passwords

When a user reuses the same password on multiple platforms, any breach on one of them can compromise the rest. This phenomenon is known as the ‚Äúdomino effect.‚Äù

Practical example: If you use the same password for your email, social media, and bank, and your email account is hacked, attackers could use that same password to access your other services.

That's why it's essential that each account has a unique password, especially those containing critical information such as your primary email, educational platforms, bank accounts, and social media accounts.

Password management tools

Remembering lots of long, secure passwords can be tricky. That's why there are password managers, which are apps or programs that store all your encrypted passwords in one place. You only need to remember one ‚Äúmaster password‚Äù to access the rest.

Advantages of using a password manager:

  • They automatically generate secure passwords.
  • They store passwords securely and encrypted.
  • They allow access from multiple devices.
  • They prevent password reuse.

Examples of reliable managers:

  • Bitwarden (free and open source)
  • 1Password
  • LastPass
  • NordPass
  • The manager built into browsers such as Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge

Using a manager is a practical and professional solution for maintaining digital security without complications.

Two-factor authentication (2FA/MFA)

Two-factor authentication (2FA) is a security method that requires two proofs to confirm the user's identity before allowing access to an account. Even if someone knows your password, they won't be able to access your account without the second step.

Most common types of second factors:

  • SMS codes: These are sent to your registered phone number.
  • Authenticator apps: Such as Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or Authy, these generate temporary codes every few seconds.
  • Biometrics: Fingerprint, facial recognition, or iris scanning, used on modern devices.

Practical example: When you log in to your email, you enter your password. You then receive a verification code on your cell phone that you must enter to complete the login process.

This system is very effective in preventing unauthorized access, even if your credentials have been leaked on the internet.

Suggested Activities:

  • Hands-on Workshop: Use the Secure Password Generator on the web to create five strong passwords and explain them.
  • Simulation: You will be sent a series of fictitious emails and must identify which ones are fake (e.g., a fake email or link).
