MODULE 5 - Social Media: Conscious and Safe Use

Objective: Properly configure privacy settings on social media, avoiding overexposure, accepting only trusted contacts, and properly managing the personal information that is shared.

Privacy settings on major social media platforms (TikTok, Instagram, X, LinkedIn)

Each social media platform offers specific tools to control who can view, comment on, or interact with your content. Learning how to use these settings reduces the risk of exposure and improves your online experience.

TikTok:

A platform focused on short videos with a wide audience reach, especially among young people. Recommended:

  • Set your account to private to approve who can follow you.
  • Limit who can comment, react, or send messages.
  • Disable the option to download your videos.
  • Use comment filters to avoid offensive language.

Instagram:

Allows you to keep your account private and control interaction with stories and posts:

  • Limit who can reply to your stories.
  • Disable your activity status.
  • Review and approve tags before they appear on your profile.
  • Automatically filter offensive messages and comments.

X (formerly Twitter):

Social network based on text and public discussions. You can:

  • Make your account private to protect your posts.
  • Control who can mention or reply to you.
  • Mute offensive accounts without blocking them.
  • Turn off location in posts.

LinkedIn:

A professional network that should be used with care so as not to expose unnecessary work or personal information:

  • Hide your email address and phone number.
  • Configure who can see your contacts or your posts.
  • Limit the visibility of your profile in external searches.
  • Enable notifications only for direct contacts.

It is advisable to review these settings at least every quarter or after each major update to the application.

Risks of overexposing personal information (location, routines, family details)

Sharing details about your daily life without filters can put you at risk without you even realizing it. Overexposure occurs when, directly or indirectly, too much personal information is revealed.

Examples of overexposure:

  • Showing where you are in real time (geolocation).
  • Making videos or photos showing your school or work uniform.
  • Posting content from inside your home, revealing your surroundings.
  • Mentioning routines, such as where and when you train, study, or work.

This data can be used by malicious individuals to harass, scam, or impersonate you.

The importance of filtering friend/follow requests

Accepting unknown followers without checking their profiles can open the door to dangers such as:

  • Theft of personal information from your profile.
  • Receiving offensive or misleading messages.
  • Creation of fake profiles that mimic your identity or that of your friends.

Recommendations:

  • Do not accept profiles with no content, generic photos, or suspicious names.
  • Check for mutual friends or interests.
  • Prefer private accounts if you share personal or sensitive content.

Carefully filtering your followers protects your digital environment and limits the reach of potential attackers or scammers.

How to report inappropriate content or fake profiles

Social networks have options for reporting offensive accounts, impersonation, harassment, or content that violates their rules. Using them helps maintain a healthier environment for everyone.

General steps for reporting:

  1. Go to the content (comment, video, or profile).
  2. Click on the options menu (usually three dots).
  3. Select ‚ÄúReport‚Äù or ‚ÄúFlag.‚Äù
  4. Explain the reason for the report (harassment, impersonation, violence, etc.).
  5. Submit the report and, if necessary, block the offensive account.

In many cases, you can also mute an account without blocking it to avoid confrontation.

Be careful with information shared by third parties!

Sharing information or images of other people without their consent can affect their privacy, security, or reputation. On social media, this can go viral in minutes.

Common examples:

  • Uploading photos of friends or family without telling them.
  • Sharing screenshots of private conversations.
  • Tagging people in sensitive situations without their approval.
  • Recording or showing third parties in your videos without their knowledge.

Recommendations:

  • Ask for permission before posting content featuring other people.
  • Avoid showing the faces of minors without authorization.
  • Do not share content received via private message without approval.
  • Acting with respect for the privacy of others is part of responsible digital behavior.

Suggested Activities:

  • Profile Review: Each participant reviews the privacy settings of one of their social media accounts.
  • Case Study: What information is safe to share on social media and what is not?
